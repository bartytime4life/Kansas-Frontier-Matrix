# Code of Conduct

Standards for participation in the Kansas Frontier Matrix (KFM) community.

**Status:** Active after contact details below are filled  
**Owners:** Repository maintainers, reviewer/stewards, governance authority  
**Quick links:** [Expected behavior](#expected-behavior) · [Restricted behavior](#restricted-behavior) · [Reporting](#reporting) · [Enforcement](#enforcement) · [Scope](#scope)

## Purpose

Kansas Frontier Matrix is built to support evidence-first, transparent, collaborative work.  
That only works when contributors, reviewers, maintainers, researchers, and community members can participate safely and respectfully.

This Code of Conduct defines the behavioral expectations for KFM spaces. It complements technical and governance rules. Where a security, privacy, licensing, sovereignty, sensitivity, or publication policy is stricter than this Code of Conduct, the stricter rule applies.

## Expected behavior

In KFM spaces, participants are expected to:

- treat other people with respect, patience, and professionalism
- engage with ideas, claims, code, data, and decisions without attacking people
- communicate clearly, especially when giving review feedback or rejecting work
- credit sources, contributors, and prior work accurately
- ask questions in good faith and help others learn project norms
- respect different viewpoints, disciplines, backgrounds, and lived experiences
- protect sensitive, private, restricted, or culturally sensitive information
- follow review boundaries, release gates, and moderation decisions
- repair harm when they cause it, including correcting inaccurate statements or contributions
- support accessible, inclusive participation in code, docs, issues, discussions, and public-facing project outputs

## KFM-specific collaboration standards

Because KFM is an evidence-first and policy-governed project, the following are core community expectations:

- Do not fabricate, overstate, or obscure evidence.
- Do not present guesses, generated text, or unverifiable claims as confirmed facts.
- Do not bypass rights, sensitivity, redaction, or publication controls.
- Do not pressure others to merge, publish, or expose material that has not passed required review.
- Do not collapse review boundaries by self-approving policy-significant work when independent review is required.
- Do not remove attribution, provenance, or other audit-critical context from contributions.
- When uncertainty exists around evidence, rights, or safety, choose the safer path and escalate for review.

## Restricted behavior

The following behavior is not allowed in KFM spaces:

- harassment, intimidation, stalking, dogpiling, or unwanted personal attention
- insults, demeaning language, character attacks, or discriminatory remarks
- sexualized comments, imagery, or conduct that is inappropriate to the community context
- threats of violence, encouragement of harm, or celebration of harm
- doxxing or sharing private, restricted, or identifying information without permission
- retaliation against someone for reporting a concern or participating in an investigation
- impersonation, deceptive identity, or attempts to evade moderation or enforcement
- plagiarism or failure to credit sources, contributors, or collaborators
- knowingly false claims about evidence, citations, dataset status, test results, benchmarks, approvals, or repository state
- attempts to expose or publish restricted, aggregate-only, or sensitive-location data outside approved workflows
- spamming, off-topic promotion, or commercial solicitation outside community norms
- disruptive conduct that repeatedly derails collaboration, review, or community safety

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all KFM community spaces, including but not limited to:

- source repositories
- pull requests, issues, commits, reviews, and discussions
- documentation, specifications, and governance artifacts
- datasets, metadata, evidence bundles, story drafts, and public-facing narratives
- chat spaces, email, meetings, workshops, and events
- official project social accounts or other places where someone is representing KFM

When making a report, include what you can:

- what happened
- where it happened
- when it happened
- who was involved
- links, screenshots, logs, or other supporting context if available
- whether there is an urgent safety or privacy risk

## Enforcement

Repository maintainers, reviewer/stewards, and governance authorities may take any action they deem necessary to protect participants and the project, including:

- requesting a change in behavior
- moderating, editing, hiding, or removing content
- rejecting contributions
- locking conversations
- restricting access to specific spaces
- suspending participation for a period of time
- permanently removing access to project spaces

Moderators will review reports as promptly and fairly as practical. They will aim to protect privacy, preserve safety, and keep a clear record of significant decisions.

## Enforcement ladder

Enforcement is context-dependent. Depending on severity, repeated lower steps may be skipped.

### 1. Correction

Used for lower-impact problems, misunderstandings, or first-time conduct issues.

Possible outcomes:

- clarification of expectations
- request to stop a behavior
- request to edit, retract, or correct content
- private or public acknowledgment of harm

### 2. Warning

Used for more serious incidents or repeated problems.

Possible outcomes:

- formal warning
- temporary communication limits
- temporary restriction from specific channels, threads, or reviews
- temporary requirement to work only through designated maintainers or moderators

### 3. Temporary suspension

Used for serious violations, repeated violations, or refusal to follow prior direction.

Possible outcomes:

- temporary loss of access to some or all project spaces
- removal from active review or publishing workflows
- temporary block on submitting new contributions

### 4. Permanent removal

Used for severe violations or repeated violations after prior enforcement.

Possible outcomes:

- permanent removal from project spaces
- permanent loss of contribution privileges
- permanent exclusion from events or official representation of the project

## Additional handling for evidence and policy violations

Because KFM handles provenance, rights, and potentially sensitive material, some violations may require immediate operational response in addition to moderation. These can include:

- reverting or removing unsafe content
- revoking access
- rotating credentials or tokens
- auditing affected artifacts, logs, or release history
- notifying stewards, custodians, or governance authorities
- documenting the incident in an ADR, governance note, or equivalent review record when required
