## Vegetation-stress knowledge character

A vegetation-stress indicator is a **derived, time-aware, spatially scoped candidate signal**. It is not a direct statement that a plant, crop, habitat, field, parcel, or ecosystem is stressed unless the underlying evidence and scope support that exact claim.

### Required classification dimensions

Every candidate must identify:

| Dimension | Required distinction |
|---|---|
| Subject | Crop, plant taxon, vegetation community, habitat class, land-cover class, ecoregion, restoration site, or aggregate area. |
| Signal | Observed condition, remote-sensing index/classification, modeled anomaly, aggregate statistic, administrative context, hazard context, or generated interpretation. |
| Stress class | Drought, heat, smoke, fire, flood, pest/disease pressure, salinity, erosion, phenology anomaly, classification anomaly, unknown, or another reviewed class. |
| Support type | Observed, modeled, aggregate, contextual, regulatory, candidate, interpreted, or generated. |
| Spatial support | Grid cell, coarse tile, county, HUC, ecoregion, habitat patch, survey unit, field candidate, or another explicit unit. |
| Temporal support | Observation interval, modeled period, valid interval, source vintage, retrieval time, processing time, release time. |
| Baseline | Explicit reference product, period, method, and version. |
| Uncertainty | Confidence, missingness, quality flags, scale limits, classification ambiguity, and reviewer caveats. |
| Evidence state | Resolved EvidenceRef/EvidenceBundle support or explicit abstention/hold. |
| Policy/release state | Independent finite decisions; never inferred from the signal. |

### Support-role rules

| Support role | Allowed claim | Prohibited upgrade |
|---|---|---|
| Observed measurement | What was measured at the stated location, scale, and time. | General ecosystem or causal truth. |
| Remote-sensing index/classification | A derived spectral/classification signal with method and resolution. | Field observation, taxon identity, cause, or management need. |
| Modeled output | Estimate, anomaly, hindcast, forecast, or scenario with model identity. | Observation or official warning. |
| Aggregate statistic | Summary at the stated aggregate unit. | Field, parcel, operator, rare-plant, or occurrence truth. |
| Administrative/regulatory context | Official-source status within its authority and effective dates. | Biological or physical observation by itself. |
| Hazard context | Driver or context from an owning Hazards source. | KFM-issued alert or life-safety instruction. |
| Generated interpretation | Bounded explanation after evidence and policy checks. | Evidence, proof, policy decision, or release authority. |

### Cause attribution

A stress indicator must not claim cause merely because contextual variables co-occur.

Cause language requires:

- a defined causal question and scope;
- evidence appropriate to that question;
- documented confounders and alternatives;
- accepted method and review;
- uncertainty and limitation statements;
- policy-safe wording;
- release approval for public use.

Otherwise, use bounded wording such as **associated context**, **coincident signal**, **possible contributor**, or `ABSTAIN`.

## Cross-domain ownership matrix

No single lane owns every part of a vegetation-stress candidate.

| Concern | Owning lane | Allowed contribution | Must remain separate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crop stress, crop observations, public aggregate agriculture indicators | Agriculture | Agriculture-specific signals and public-safe aggregates | Private field/operator conditions, yield/loss determinations, prescriptions |
| Plant taxonomy, specimens, occurrences, vegetation communities, rare-plant sensitivity | Flora | Botanical subject and evidence context | Exact sensitive locations, occurrence truth from modeled surfaces |
| Land cover, habitat class, patches, condition, suitability, uncertainty | Habitat | Landscape and condition context | Species/plant occurrence and regulatory designation truth |
| Map units, components, horizons, substrate, soil-moisture context | Soil | Scale-aware substrate/support context | Crop, habitat, or botanical truth |
| Watersheds, gauges, water observations, wetland/riparian/flood context | Hydrology | Water and watershed context | Regulatory flood context relabeled as observed flooding |
| Weather, heat, smoke, aerosol, modeled and observed atmosphere | Atmosphere | Driver/context signals with freshness and model/observation separation | Official advisories or life-safety guidance |
| Drought, fire, storm, heat, warning and declaration context | Hazards | Official-source context with effective times | KFM emergency alerting, declarations, or safety instruction |

## Rights, sensitivity, security, and data minimization

### Derived-risk rule

Sensitivity is evaluated on the **derived output**, not only on each input.

A public input can become restricted when joined with:

- rare, protected, or culturally sensitive Flora context;
- sensitive habitat or species context;
- private field, operator, parcel, ownership, or yield context;
- archaeology, burial-adjacent, or cultural knowledge;
- infrastructure location or vulnerability context;
- precise hydrology, restoration, stewardship, or monitoring context;
- small-cell temporal patterns;
- model outputs that enable reconstruction.

Apply the most restrictive relevant policy.

### Default responses

| Risk | Default |
|---|---|
| Exact rare-flora or sensitive-habitat geometry | Redact, generalize, restrict, hold, or deny. |

| Unsupported causal interpretation | Abstain or narrow language. |
| Over-precise public raster/vector surface | Aggregate, coarsen, suppress, delay, restrict, or deny. |
| Sensitive change diff | Minimize or suppress the diff; do not expose reconstructive clues. |
| Credentials or private endpoints | Never commit or log. |

- one spec and one consumer are bound;
- source activation and allowed roles resolve;
- candidate contract and schema validate;
- baseline, method, thresholds, uncertainty, and materiality are explicit;
- deterministic no-network fixtures and substantive tests pass;
- finite outcomes and reason codes are stable;
- sensitive joins, private fields, rare flora, and over-precise geometry fail closed;
- receipts and safe observability are emitted;
- correction, withdrawal, deactivation, and rollback are tested;
- CI is substantive and activation-aware;
- production use is separately approved and monitored.

### Future public product

A public product additionally requires:

- evidence closure;
- rights and sensitivity approval;
- public-safe representation;
- catalog/triplet consistency where applicable;
- human review;
- release manifest;
- correction and rollback support;
- governed API/UI/map delivery;
- public caveats and time state;
- AI citation and abstention behavior.
