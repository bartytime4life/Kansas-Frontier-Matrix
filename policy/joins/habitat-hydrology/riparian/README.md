# Habitat–Hydrology Riparian Join Admissibility Policy Boundary

> **One-line purpose.** `policy/joins/habitat-hydrology/riparian/` documents how KFM decides whether a riparian relationship between Habitat-owned products and Hydrology-owned features or observations is admissible for a named operation and audience—without becoming Habitat truth, Hydrology truth, a wetland or floodplain determination, a relation schema, a validator, an alert, a release decision, or a publication path.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Riparian admissibility is not riparian truth.** This lane may determine whether an explicitly declared relationship may be used for one bounded purpose. It cannot prove that a habitat is riparian, prove hydrologic connectivity, determine wetland jurisdiction, establish a floodplain boundary, prove current inundation, transfer authority between domains, create evidence, or approve release.

> [!CAUTION]
> **Proximity is not hydrologic connectivity.** A Habitat polygon touching or lying near a stream, wetland, floodplain, reach, or modeled moisture surface does not by itself establish riparian function, water dependence, current hydrologic support, flood exposure, legal status, or ecological condition.

> [!WARNING]
> **KFM is not a regulatory or life-safety authority.** This lane must not produce wetland delineations, jurisdictional determinations, flood insurance or permitting conclusions, emergency alerts, evacuation guidance, water-allocation advice, private access conclusions, or management prescriptions.

## Purpose

`policy/joins/habitat-hydrology/riparian/` exists to answer one bounded policy-routing question:

> Given explicit Habitat and Hydrology endpoint references, a declared riparian relation profile, source roles, evidence, hydrologic identity and version, temporal and spatial scope, topology, uncertainty, rights, sensitivity, requested operation, audience, surface, lifecycle state, review state, release state, correction lineage, and evaluator context, may the riparian join be used—and under which enforceable obligations?

A mature riparian pair policy should:

- preserve independent Habitat and Hydrology authority;
- require evidence for both endpoints and for the relation assertion;
- distinguish stream or wetland context from observed flow, modeled conditions, and regulatory flood context;
- preserve hydrography version, feature identity, reach identity, observation time, model time, and geometry provenance;
- distinguish adjacency, intersection, catchment membership, floodplain context, moisture support, and demonstrated hydrologic connectivity;
- prevent regulatory, legal, emergency, or management conclusions from being inferred from contextual joins;
- apply the most restrictive applicable rights, sensitivity, and audience posture;
- route sensitive ecological or private-land implications through accepted policy and review;
- normalize required policy-family decisions into one finite caller result;
- preserve correction, source refresh, stale-state, withdrawal, and rollback dependencies;
- keep public maps, graphs, search, export, screenshots, vector indexes, and AI behind governed interfaces.

This README is a routing and review contract. It does not activate policy.

## Scope and bounded context

### In scope

This lane covers policy decisions for declared Habitat–Hydrology riparian relationships such as:

- HabitatPatch associated with a Hydrology-owned stream, river, wetland, lake edge, reservoir edge, or related HydroFeature;
- habitat-support interpretation derived from Hydrology-owned moisture, wetland, floodplain-context, network, or observation evidence;
- riparian corridor or connectivity context that references Hydrology-owned features without copying their authority;
- restoration-opportunity context where Hydrology evidence is one governed input;
- map, graph, search, export, analytical, review, or AI use of an already declared riparian relation;
- public-safe representation of the relation after evidence, policy, review, and release gates close;
- correction, stale-state, source-version change, withdrawal, invalidation, and rollback affecting a riparian derivative.

### Out of scope

- defining Habitat or Hydrology objects;
- declaring a location legally riparian, wetland, floodplain, floodway, navigable, jurisdictional, or regulated;
- creating HydroFeature, ReachIdentity, HUCUnit, HabitatPatch, EcologicalSystem, observation, model, or regulatory records;
- computing stream networks, hydrologic models, flood forecasts, or wetland delineations;
- deciding source identity, source role, rights, or evidence truth;
- implementing buffers, intersection, network traversal, moisture models, or remote sensing;
- issuing emergency, flood, drought, water-supply, permitting, agricultural, land-management, or restoration instructions;
- inferring private land access, ownership, residence, operator identity, or legal obligations;
- approving promotion, release, publication, correction, or rollback;
- storing exact restricted locations or confidential records;
- using an AI-generated explanation as relation evidence.

### Operation-specific use

A relation may be admissible for one operation and inadmissible for another.

| Operation | Typical posture |
|---|---|
| Internal candidate review using synthetic/public-safe inputs | May be allowed with audit and no-export obligations. |
| Habitat classification using released HydroFeature context | May be allowed if relation meaning, version, evidence, and role checks close. |
| Public map of coarse released riparian habitat context | May be allowed after policy, review, release, and public-surface tests. |
| Precise join exposing protected ecological or private-land context | Deny or require restricted steward review. |
| Wetland-jurisdiction or flood-insurance conclusion | Deny; outside KFM authority. |
| Emergency flood or evacuation guidance | Deny; outside KFM authority. |
| AI claim that proximity proves hydrologic support | Abstain or deny. |

## Domain ownership boundary

### Habitat owns

Habitat retains authority for Habitat-owned products such as:

- `HabitatPatch`;
- `EcologicalSystem`;
- habitat-support interpretations;
- Habitat Quality Score;
- SuitabilityModel and its model lineage;
- ConnectivityEdge and Corridor;
- Restoration Opportunity;
- StewardshipZone;
- Habitat-owned uncertainty, caveats, and correction lineage.

### Hydrology owns

Hydrology retains authority for Hydrology-owned products such as:

- `HydroFeature`;
- `ReachIdentity`;
- stream, river, lake, wetland, reservoir, canal, and related network/context geometry;
- `HUCUnit` and watershed accounting context;
- GaugeSite and observed flow, stage, level, or water-quality observations;
- Hydrograph and modeled/observed series with role labels;
- NFHL or other regulatory flood context;
- observed flood-event evidence;
- network traversal and upstream/downstream context;
- hydrologic source version, topology, time, uncertainty, and correction lineage.

### The relation may own only a relationship assertion

A future accepted neutral relation object may describe a bounded relationship such as:

- intersects;
- adjacent to;
- within declared riparian context;
- associated with a Hydrology-owned feature under an accepted method;
- supported by a named observation or modeled context;
- inside a catchment or flood-context unit for one stated analytical purpose.

It must not become the canonical record for either endpoint.

### Ownership non-transfer rules

1. Habitat does not acquire Hydrology source authority by consuming a HydroFeature.
2. Hydrology does not acquire Habitat classification or suitability authority by intersecting a HabitatPatch.
3. A relation does not turn a modeled hydrologic surface into an observation.
4. A relation does not turn regulatory flood context into observed inundation.
5. A relation does not turn proximity into ecological function.
6. A public layer does not become the source record for either domain.
7. A graph edge does not become canonical truth.
8. A review does not transfer domain ownership.
9. A release manifest does not repair an unsupported relation.
10. AI prose cannot create or strengthen the relation.

## Concept separation

The following distinctions are mandatory.

| Concept A | Concept B | Required separation |
|---|---|---|
| HydroFeature | ReachIdentity | Generic source/network feature is not stable reach identity across vintages. |
| Reach or stream proximity | Hydrologic connectivity | Spatial closeness does not prove connected flow or water support. |
| Wetland context | Jurisdictional wetland determination | Contextual mapping is not a legal delineation. |
| NFHL regulatory area | Observed flood event | Regulatory designation is not observed inundation. |
| Observed flow/stage | Modeled hydrograph | Observation and model roles remain explicit. |
| HabitatPatch | Riparian relation | Habitat object is not the relationship assertion. |
| Riparian relation | Riparian function | A relation profile may describe context without proving ecological function. |
| Floodplain overlap | Flood exposure truth | Overlap may be contextual and time/version dependent. |
| Moisture support | Current water availability | Historical/model context is not current observed condition. |
| Public HydroFeature geometry | Public-safe riparian derivative | Each needs its own release and evidence posture. |
| Validation pass | Policy permission | Shape/topology checks do not grant use. |
| Policy permission | Release approval | An admissible operation is not publication approval. |
| Pipeline result | EvidenceBundle | A computation is not proof closure. |
| Regulatory context | Emergency guidance | KFM is not alerting or life-safety authority. |
| Restoration opportunity | Management instruction | Analytical opportunity is not a prescription. |
| Parcel overlap | Access or ownership | Geometry does not establish legal rights or private access. |

1. **Ownership remains separate.**
2. **Source role remains fixed and visible.**
3. **Hydrologic identity and source version remain explicit.**
4. **Proximity never silently becomes connectivity or causality.**
5. **Observed, modeled, regulatory, aggregate, administrative, candidate, and synthetic contexts do not collapse.**

13. **Regulatory and life-safety conclusions fail closed.**
14. **Private-land, protected-location, and infrastructure implications are minimized or denied.**
15. **Corrections and source refreshes propagate to every derivative and cache.**
16. **AI answers cite released evidence or abstain.**
17. **Denied and abstained attempts remain auditable without leaking protected detail.**
18. **Every change remains correctable and reversible.**

Every requested use must pass all applicable checks.

### Check 1 — Domain ownership

Verify that Habitat and Hydrology objects retain their owners and that the proposed derivative has one explicit owner or neutral relation owner.

**Block when:** the join copies Hydrology truth into Habitat authority or vice versa.

### Check 2 — Endpoint identity and version

Verify stable endpoint references, source IDs, object versions, spec hashes, hydrography vintages, and geometry versions.

**Block when:** a stream/reach/wetland is named only by text, geometry, or stale alias.

### Check 3 — Relation profile and predicate

Require an accepted relation meaning that distinguishes adjacency, intersection, catchment membership, flood-context overlap, moisture support, and demonstrated connectivity.

**Block when:** a generic “riparian” flag has no contract, method, or evidence.

### Check 4 — Hydrology source role

Preserve observed, modeled, regulatory, aggregate, administrative, candidate, and synthetic roles.

**Block when:** NFHL/regulatory context becomes observed flood, model output becomes observation, or candidate data becomes release-ready.

### Check 5 — Habitat product role

Preserve observed Habitat inputs, modeled suitability, derived corridors, restoration opportunities, and uncertainty.

**Block when:** a Habitat model is presented as observed ecological condition or guaranteed riparian function.

### Check 6 — Spatial and topology validity

Validate CRS, geometry validity, spatial operation, scale, network/topology context, and method-specific tolerances through accepted validators.

**Block when:** proximity, buffering, snapping, or intersection fabricates identity or connectivity.

### Check 7 — Temporal and freshness alignment

Verify observation time, model valid time, source vintage, season, hydrologic regime, retrieval time, release time, and stale-state posture.

**Block when:** old hydrography, historic flood context, or modeled moisture is presented as current support without qualification.

### Check 8 — Endpoint and relation evidence

Require resolvable evidence for each endpoint and independently for the relation assertion.

**Block when:** evidence closes only one side or the relation exists solely because a pipeline produced it.

### Check 9 — Rights and access

Verify source terms, redistribution, derived-use permission, citation, access restrictions, and audience.

**Block when:** public use would exceed source or derivative rights.

### Check 10 — Sensitivity and composition risk

Evaluate the produced relation and all downstream surfaces for protected ecology, private land, infrastructure, archaeology/cultural knowledge, and reconstruction risk.

**Block when:** composition reveals more than either source exposed safely.

### Check 11 — Regulatory, legal, and life-safety boundary

Reject wetland jurisdiction, floodplain certification, insurance, permitting, navigability, allocation, emergency, evacuation, or management claims.

**Block when:** contextual evidence is used as authoritative determination or instruction.

### Check 12 — Review, release, correction, and rollback

Require appropriate review, release support, dependency registration, correction path, cache invalidation, and rollback target.

**Block when:** a validator pass or candidate artifact is treated as publication approval.

### Composition rule

A successful subset is insufficient. One blocking failure produces a finite negative outcome.

```text
explicit request
  -> endpoint identity and ownership
  -> declared relation profile
  -> source-role checks
  -> spatial/topology checks
  -> temporal/freshness checks
  -> endpoint + relation evidence
  -> rights + sensitivity + authority boundary
  -> review/release/correction dependencies
  -> composed finite outcome
```

## Hydrology source-role boundary

Hydrology source role is load-bearing for riparian interpretation.

| Hydrology context | Allowed interpretation | Forbidden collapse |
|---|---|---|
| Observed flow/stage/water level | Time-bounded observed hydrologic evidence. | Permanent riparian support or regulatory status. |
| HydroFeature from reviewed hydrography | Network/context geometry with explicit version. | Current flow observation, legal channel boundary, or reach identity without support. |
| ReachIdentity | Stable reach identity across accepted vintages. | Any ecological or flow condition by itself. |
| Modeled hydrograph/moisture/inundation | Modeled context with uncertainty and valid time. | Observed condition or guaranteed habitat function. |
| Regulatory flood zone | Regulatory/contextual flood designation. | Observed flood event or current inundation. |
| HUC/watershed aggregate | Accounting/aggregation context. | Per-site or per-patch truth. |
| Administrative water record | Administrative context. | Observed hydrology, access right, ownership, or allocation advice. |
| Candidate or watcher record | Review input only. | Published evidence or authoritative relation. |
| Synthetic/AI representation | Interpretive carrier only. | Admitted source or observed evidence. |

### Signature role-collapse denials

- NFHL or other regulatory flood context labeled as observed flood;
- modeled hydrograph labeled as gauge observation;
- HUC aggregate cited as a patch-level condition;
- stream-network geometry treated as current flowing water;
- administrative record treated as field observation;
- candidate record exposed publicly;
- generated summary treated as EvidenceBundle support.

## Temporal, spatial, and topology boundary

### Temporal requirements

A riparian relation is time-aware.

Require, where applicable:

- source vintage;
- observation time;
- model issue and valid time;
- season or hydrologic period;
- retrieval time;
- relation evaluation time;
- review and release time;
- correction and withdrawal time.

Temporal mismatch must remain visible. A habitat classification derived from one season and hydrology from another may be invalid or require review.

### Spatial requirements

Require:

- explicit CRS and geography version;
- geometry source and version;
- relation operation;
- scale/resolution;
- topology/network context;
- uncertainty and positional limitations;
- public-safe geometry posture;
- no silent coordinate snapping.

### Topology requirements

A geometric intersection may not prove hydrologic network connectivity.

Examples:

- a HabitatPatch crossing a mapped stream line may support adjacency, not necessarily functional dependence;
- a polygon within a floodplain may support flood-context overlap, not observed inundation;
- a wetland feature near a stream may require separate evidence for surface or groundwater connection;
- a corridor parallel to a river may be landscape context without riparian classification;
- a habitat polygon inside a HUC is watershed membership, not reach-level support.

### Scale compatibility

Block or review when:

- coarse modeled hydrology is joined to fine Habitat geometry as if equally precise;
- an aggregate is projected onto a single patch;
- different hydrography vintages or geometries are silently mixed;
- public generalization is treated as canonical geometry;
- output precision exceeds evidence precision.

## Composition and inference risk

A riparian join can create a fact that neither endpoint disclosed alone.

### Ecological inference risk

Potential risks include:

- inferring protected species or rare-plant locations from riparian habitat;
- reconstructing sensitive habitat through stream, wetland, corridor, or restoration surfaces;
- revealing private conservation or stewardship activity;
- exposing culturally sensitive or archaeological patterns near waterways;
- converting coarse habitat context into precise site inference.

### Private-land risk

A stream/patch/parcel composition may imply:

- access routes;
- landowner identity;
- private water use;
- operator activity;
- residence or asset location;
- restoration or conservation participation.

Those implications are not ordinary public join outputs.

### Infrastructure risk

Hydrology and riparian context may reveal:

- water-control structures;
- intake or discharge context;
- critical crossing or access points;
- vulnerable infrastructure relationships.

Infrastructure-sensitive combinations must route to the applicable restricted policy and review.

### Repeated-release risk

Even generalized releases may leak detail through:

- differencing across versions;
- zoom-dependent changes;
- tile boundaries;
- temporal updates;
- search filters;
- graph-neighborhood traversal;
- export joins;
- AI synthesis across released products.

Policy must evaluate the series, not only one artifact.

### Derived-product risk

The riparian-vegetation pipeline documentation confirms multiple adjacent implementation homes and no established active implementation. A produced candidate must not be assumed safe or canonical because it came from a named pipeline.

## Regulatory and life-safety boundary

This lane has **no authority** to issue or imply:

- jurisdictional wetland delineation;
- Waters of the United States or equivalent legal status;
- floodplain/floodway certification;
- flood-insurance determination;
- building, development, permitting, or zoning conclusion;
- navigability or public-access conclusion;
- water right, allocation, withdrawal, or priority conclusion;
- dam, levee, bridge, road, or utility safety assessment;
- current flood warning, watch, evacuation, rescue, or emergency guidance;
- restoration, grazing, agriculture, engineering, or land-management instruction;
- compliance certification.

### Required public disclaimers

Where a public-safe contextual product is allowed, it should communicate that:

- the product is contextual and evidence-bounded;
- source roles and vintages matter;
- modeled or regulatory context is not observed condition;
- geometry is not a legal boundary;
- the product is not emergency or professional guidance;
- authoritative agencies and qualified professionals control regulatory and life-safety decisions.

A disclaimer cannot convert a denied product into an allowed one.

## Riparian posture model

OPEN / STEWARD-REVIEW / DENIED are proposed governance postures, not current `PolicyDecision.outcome` values.

### OPEN

Potentially appropriate only when all required checks pass and:

- both endpoints are released and public-safe;
- source roles and versions are explicit;
- the relation profile is accepted;
- the use is contextual, not regulatory, legal, life-safety, private-land, or instructional;
- spatial and temporal scales are compatible;
- no protected ecology or reconstruction risk is present;
- obligations are enforceable;
- release and rollback support exists.

Example:

> A released, coarse HabitatPatch summary associated with a released, versioned HydroFeature for a general educational map, with relation evidence and no sensitive implication.

### STEWARD-REVIEW

Required when one or more apply:

- novel relation profile;
- modeled support affecting consequential interpretation;
- mixed source roles or scale;
- temporal mismatch requiring expert judgment;
- sensitive ecological, private-land, cultural, or infrastructure context;
- restoration-opportunity or corridor use;
- repeated-release or reconstruction risk;
- source or rights ambiguity that may be resolvable;
- restricted audience;
- pair policy or relation contract remains proposed.

### DENIED

Required when one or more apply:

- endpoint or relation evidence is absent;
- source role is collapsed;
- relation profile is missing or unsupported;
- proximity is presented as proven connectivity or causality;
- regulatory flood context is presented as observed flood;
- output makes jurisdictional, legal, insurance, emergency, or management claims;
- public output exposes protected ecology, private land, restricted infrastructure, or sensitive cultural context;
- a candidate, quarantined, restricted, withdrawn, or stale input is used without a governed path;
- caller cannot enforce obligations;
- release or rollback support is absent for public use;
- evaluator integrity fails.

### Novel joins default to review

Until an accepted register says otherwise, a new riparian profile should not silently become OPEN. The safe default is STEWARD-REVIEW or a canonical negative outcome.

## Public surface controls

### Map and tile

- Serve released derivative geometry only.
- Never treat style hiding as policy.
- Surface source role, version, model/regulatory status, and limitations where material.
- Prevent zoom or tile inspection from exceeding approved precision.
- Re-check release and correction state.
- Invalidate tiles after source or policy changes.
- Do not render a regulatory flood layer as observed event evidence.
- Do not imply wetland jurisdiction or legal boundary.

### Graph and triplet

- Preserve endpoint ownership and relation profile.
- Keep the relation assertion distinct from endpoint objects.
- Deny graph traversals that reconstruct protected ecology, private land, or infrastructure detail.
- Do not promote graph degree or adjacency into causal truth.
- Invalidate dependent edges after corrections.

### Search and index

- Index only released public-safe fields.
- Do not make internal IDs, restricted aliases, or precise geometry searchable.
- Prevent filters or facets from narrowing protected context.
- Invalidate indexes after withdrawal or increased restriction.

### Export

- Re-evaluate operation, audience, quantity, precision, and downstream rights.
- Include provenance, source roles, time, version, limitations, and release references.
- Deny bulk extraction that creates new inference risk.
- Do not export internal policy, registry, or restricted geometry.

### Screenshots and reports

- Treat static images as derivative releases.
- Apply the same precision, labeling, and sensitivity obligations.
- Prevent screenshots from bypassing map controls.
- Record or reference the released source artifact.

### Vector and embedding systems

- Do not index restricted relation details.
- Preserve policy and release state outside the embedding.
- Invalidate derived indexes after correction.
- Do not let semantic similarity recover denied relations.

### AI and Focus Mode

AI may:

- summarize released public-safe riparian context;
- distinguish observed, modeled, regulatory, and aggregate support;
- explain uncertainty and limitations;
- cite the applicable EvidenceBundle;
- abstain when relation support does not close.

AI must not:

- infer hydrologic connectivity from map proximity;
- infer current conditions from old or modeled context;
- treat regulatory flood zones as observed floods;
- determine wetland jurisdiction;
- provide emergency or management instructions;
- infer private access, ownership, or protected ecological locations;
- combine released fragments to reconstruct denied detail;
- present generated language as evidence.

## Correction, revocation, withdrawal, and rollback

### Triggers

Re-evaluate or withdraw when:

- Habitat endpoint is corrected or superseded;
- HydroFeature or ReachIdentity changes;
- hydrography vintage changes;
- observation or model is corrected;
- source role changes through a new admitted descriptor;
- rights or terms change;
- sensitivity increases;
- relation contract or method changes;
- topology or geometry validation changes;
- review is revoked;
- release is withdrawn;
- a new inference or leakage risk is discovered;
- policy or evaluator version changes.

### Required cascade

A blocking change should propagate to:

- relation records;
- Habitat derivatives;
- Hydrology references;
- catalog/triplet projections;
- graph edges;
- map layers and tiles;
- search indexes;
- exports;
- screenshots and reports where managed;
- vector/embedding indexes;
- AI caches and receipts;
- release candidates and manifests.

### Correction rules

- Do not silently edit immutable decision or receipt history.
- Supersede with explicit references.
- Recompute policy with current inputs.
- Preserve prior evidence and reason for correction.
- Restrict immediately when risk increases.
- Do not restore public exposure until all gates close again.

### Rollback rules

Rollback must identify:

- affected release;
- prior safe artifact;
- dependency graph;
- invalidation targets;
- cache purge targets;
- reviewer and operator;
- verification evidence;
- completion time;
- unresolved residual risk.

A Git revert of this README affects documentation only. Operational rollback requires governed release and data actions.
