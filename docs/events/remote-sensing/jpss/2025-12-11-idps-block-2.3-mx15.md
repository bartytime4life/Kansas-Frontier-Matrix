## 2. Event Snapshot

**Event ID:** `jpss-idps-block2.3-mx15-transition`  
**Time (UTC):** `2025-12-11T16:30:00Z`  
**Authority:** NOAA Office of Satellite and Product Operations (OSPO)  
**System:** JPSS Interface Data Processing Segment (IDPS)  
**Change:** Deployment of **Block 2.3 Mx15** algorithm bundle into operations  
**KFM Status:** **Effective calibration epoch boundary** for impacted JPSS products  

Mx15 had been **delayed multiple times** due to upstream and downstream operational dependencies. For KFM, this transition marks the start of a **new calibration epoch** for affected streams and must be modeled as such in ETL, catalogs, and graph entities.

## 3. Operational Impact Summary

### 3.1 Product Classes Affected

- **ATMS SDR/TDR**
  - Updated calibration coefficients and ancillary parameters.
  - Revised geolocation and Earth-location corrections.
  - Stabilization of brightness temperature computation for selected channels.

- **VIIRS SDR/EDR chains (selected products)**
  - Minor parameter and threshold updates.
  - Adjustments to **metadata packing** and quality-flag semantics.

- **Cross-sensor consistency**
  - Harmonized shared lookup tables across sensors.
  - Timing refinements in **granule production windows** and quality flags.

### 3.2 Implications for KFM

For KFM remote-sensing ingestion:

- Calibration baselines **before vs. after** `2025-12-11T16:30Z` **must not be treated as identical**.
- STAC and DCAT records for affected products require:
  - **Algorithm version fields** reflecting `"IDPS Block2.3 Mx15"`.
  - A **calibration epoch boundary** at the event time.
- Provenance and graph models must expose the **algorithm update** as a first-class entity, allowing:
  - Cross-epoch comparison.
  - Story Nodes that explicitly reference the transition.
