## 1. Purpose and role

ôÏ24.1 is the source-role doctrine for the whole project. KFM treats source role as a **first-class identity attribute**: an observed reading is not interchangeable with a modeled estimate, a regulatory determination is not interchangeable with an administrative compilation, an aggregate publication is not interchangeable with candidate evidence, and synthetic content is never the same thing as observed reality. The lifecycle and the governed API both **fail closed** when these roles are conflated.

This file is the **navigation carrier for ôÏ24.1**, paired with the receipt-catalog carrier (ôÏ24.2) and the pipeline-gate-reference carrier (ôÏ24.6). It exists because:

- ôÏ24.1 is referenced from every prior session-authored carrier: `receipt-catalog.md` ôÏ3 (`SourceDescriptor` is the first receipt class and consumes the seven-role enum); `pipeline-gate-reference.md` ôÏ3 (Admission Gate enforces source-role assignment); `domains-v1.1.md` ôÏ6 (source-role posture table); `domains-v1.1-ch14.md` ôÏ13 (administrative-as-observation anti-collapse for Settlements specifically). Without a ôÏ24.1 carrier, those references trace back to the full-text Markdown conversion or the source PDFs. A dedicated carrier reduces navigation cost.
- ôÏ24.1 is the **highest-leverage** Chapter 24 section without a companion carrier. Receipt-catalog (ôÏ24.2) and pipeline-gate-reference (ôÏ24.6) cover the operational machinery; ôÏ24.1 covers the **identity discipline** that makes the machinery's outputs trustworthy.
- The session-authored anti-collapse content in `domains-v1.1-ch14.md` ôÏ13 (administrative-as-observation), `maplibre-master.md` ôÏ9 (rendering-related anti-patterns), and the receipt-catalog's `RedactionReceipt` / `AggregationReceipt` discipline all depend on the seven-role enum being canonical somewhere in `docs/atlases/`.

**This file is not authority.** Two non-collapse rules apply:

1. **Atlas v1.1 ôÏ24.1 wins on wording.** Where this carrier paraphrases, the section is authoritative.
2. **Chapter 24 registers are navigational aids, not authority.** Per Atlas v1.1's own ôÏ24 preamble: *"A Chapter 24 table that disagrees with v1.0 is treated as a drift entry, not as a correction."* That rule applies one level out: where this carrier disagrees with ôÏ24.1, drift is filed to `docs/registers/DRIFT_REGISTER.md`.

## 4. ôÏ24.1.1 Canonical source-role classes

> **Source:** Atlas v1.1 ôÏ24.1.1. All rows `CONFIRMED doctrine`. Reproduced verbatim from the source.

| Role | Definition (`CONFIRMED doctrine`) | Typical example | Allowed downstream role | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| **Synthetic** | Content generated by simulation, reconstruction, AI, or interpolation that has no underlying first-hand observation. ### 4.1 Reading note (ôÏ24.1.1, end of section)

> **`CONFIRMED doctrine`** *(reproduced verbatim)*: the role of a source is set at admission (`SourceDescriptor`) and is **preserved through every promotion**. Promotion does **not** upgrade an observation to a regulation, or a model to an aggregate, or a candidate to a verified record ã those are separate governed transitions with their own evidence and review requirements. `[ENCY] [DIRRULES]`

This reading note is the **central operational rule** of ôÏ24.1. Three consequences worth pinning:

1. **Role immutability at admission.** Once `SourceDescriptor.source_role` is set, it cannot be edited in place. Corrections produce a new descriptor + `CorrectionNotice` (per ôÏ6 below).
2. **Promotion ã  relabeling.** The lifecycle invariant (`RAW ã WORK / QUARANTINE ã PROCESSED ã CATALOG / TRIPLET ã PUBLISHED`) moves a record between phases; it never moves a record between roles.
3. **Role transitions are separately governed.** Upgrading a `candidate` to a verified `observed` record (for example) requires its own evidence + review process, separate from any phase-level promotion gate.

## 6. ôÏ24.1.3 Roles to `SourceDescriptor` fields (PROPOSED)

### 6.2 Proposed descriptor surface (illustrative, not authoritative)

| Field | Type / vocabulary | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| `source_role` | enum: `observed` \| `regulatory` \| `modeled` \| `aggregate` \| `administrative` \| `candidate` \| `synthetic` | **MUST** | Set at admission. **Never edited in-place**; corrections must produce a new descriptor and a `CorrectionNotice`. |

| `role_aggregation_unit` | geometry-scope token (`county`, `HUC`, `tract`, `year`, `decade`, etc.) | **MUST** when `source_role = aggregate` | Prevents geometry-scope drift on join. |
| `role_model_run_ref` | `EvidenceRef` ã `ModelRunReceipt` | **MUST** when `source_role = modeled` | Pins the inputs, parameters, and version that produced the value. |
| `role_synthetic_basis` | structured: `{ method, inputs, reality_boundary_note_ref }` | **MUST** when `source_role = synthetic` | Records what is and is not real in the carrier. |
| `role_candidate_disposition` | enum: `pending` \| `merged` \| `rejected` \| `quarantined` | **MUST** when `source_role = candidate` | Tracks promotion state; `PUBLISHED` edge forbidden until merged. |

> `NEEDS VERIFICATION`: implementation of these fields in the mounted `SourceDescriptor` schema.

### 6.3 Why each field exists

The descriptor surface is not arbitrary. Each field operationalizes a specific anti-collapse failure mode from ôÏ5:

| Field | Failure mode it operationalizes |
|---|---|
| `source_role` (always required) | All seven failure modes in ôÏ5 ã every DENY decision starts here. |
| `role_authority` | "Regulatory zone labeled as observed" (banner attribution); "Administrative compilation cited as observation" (issuing body disambiguation); "Modeled product labeled as observed" (model identity). |
| `role_aggregation_unit` | "Aggregate cited as per-place truth" ã the geometry-scope token is what the validator checks against on join. |
| `role_model_run_ref` | "Modeled product labeled as observed" ã `ModelRunReceipt` pins inputs, parameters, version; without it the model-product is unsafe to publish. |
| `role_synthetic_basis` | "Synthetic content presented as observed reality" ã `reality_boundary_note_ref` is what surfaces in the Evidence Drawer. |
| `role_candidate_disposition` | "Candidate record exposed on public surface" ã `PUBLISHED` edge is forbidden unless disposition is `merged`. |

### 8.3 Drift-detection signals

Two operational signals indicate source-role drift requiring `DRIFT_REGISTER.md` entry:
