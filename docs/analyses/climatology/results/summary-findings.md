## üìä Major Insights & Implications

- **Agriculture & Water Systems**: Rising temperatures, combined with precipitation variability, will likely shorten wheat and other grain growing seasons, reduce yields, and increase irrigation demand.  
- **Ecosystem Services**: Drought-stress and shifting precipitation regimes threaten grassland and wetland ecosystems in western Kansas, reducing their capacity for carbon sequestration and biodiversity support.  
- **Infrastructure & Resilience**: Spikes in precipitation and changing snow‚Äêmelt/streamflow dynamics increase flood risk, requiring updates in planning for hydrology, agriculture, and rural infrastructure.  
- **Equity & Consent**: Indigenous and historically underserved communities are more vulnerable to climate shifting conditions; FAIR+CARE governance ensures that data integration and modeling treat their knowledge, rights, and data sovereignty with full respect.  
- **Research & Monitoring**: The identified trends reinforce the need for continued high-resolution monitoring, improved seasonal forecast capability, and deeper coupling of climate models with land-use, hydrology and ecology modules.

## üìå Recommendations for Next Steps

- Expand high-resolution temporal modelling to sub-county (HUC8) scale, enabling localized adaptation strategies.  
- Enhance integration with cross-domain modules (hydrology, ecology, land-use) to quantify cascading impacts and feedback loops.  
- Incorporate further community-led and Indigenous-governed data streams to ensure authority-to-control and equitable modeling.  
- Monitor energy & carbon usage of modeling workflows, progressively lowering the telemetry baselines in subsequent runs.  
- Package key figures and interactive dashboards for public dissemination, maintaining accessibility and FAIR+CARE traceability.
